## The Remote Work Paradox

Most remote destinations force you to choose: beauty *or* connectivity. A stunning beach with no signal. A mountain retreat where the power cuts out. A jungle cabin where uploading a file takes all afternoon.

Sapzurro sits in one of the most isolated corners of Colombia — no roads in, no roads out, surrounded by the Darién jungle and the Caribbean Sea. By every logic, it should have the worst internet in the country.

Instead, we built the opposite.

### ⚡ 1. BatX Backup Batteries — Power That Never Stops

![BatX backup battery system at Cabaña Sancibrian](/images/batx.webp)

Sapzurro runs on a municipal generator that, like most small Caribbean towns, has scheduled downtime and occasional failures. For most accommodations in the region, when the power goes out, everything stops.

Not at Sancibrian.

We installed a **BatX battery backup system** — a high-capacity energy storage solution that charges during grid hours and seamlessly takes over the moment grid power drops. The switchover is automatic and nearly instantaneous. You won't notice it happened.

One important detail: **air conditioning runs on town grid power**. When the BatX batteries kick in, personal fans keep you comfortable — more than enough given Sapzurro's constant coastal breeze. Either way, you stay cool and connected.

**What this means for your workday:**

| Scenario | Most places in Sapzurro | Cabaña Sancibrian |
|----------|------------------------|-------------------|
| Nightly generator shutdown | Everything off | Fully powered |
| Unexpected grid failure | Blackout | No interruption |
| Long video call | Risk of cutoff mid-call | Stable to the end |
| Charging devices overnight | Not possible | Always available |
| Working at 2 AM | Dark and offline | Coffee and full power |
| Cooling | Fans only | AC on grid · fans on battery |

BatX batteries are made in Colombia, designed for exactly this kind of off-grid reliability — and they are the reason guests regularly tell us: *"I actually got more done here than in my regular office."*

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does the Starlink work during rain?**
Minor rain has no effect. During heavy tropical downpours, you may see brief dips (5–10 minutes maximum). In practice, this rarely affects a full workday.

**Can I connect multiple devices?**
Yes. The WiFi network handles multiple simultaneous connections — laptop, phone, and tablet at the same time without noticeable slowdown.

**Is there a desk in the cabin?**
Yes. The Captain's Cabin includes a spacious, dedicated work desk. Many guests prefer working from the cabin itself; others come to the Hangar Café for the social atmosphere.

**What if I need to print something or use a scanner?**
We can help coordinate this — let us know in advance and we'll find a solution.

**Are there phone calls that need quiet?**
The Hangar Café has a calm, low-noise environment. For calls requiring complete privacy, the cabin itself offers total quiet.

**Is the 24/7 power truly uninterrupted?**
The BatX system is designed for seamless automatic switchover. In practice, guests notice no interruption even during scheduled generator shutdowns.

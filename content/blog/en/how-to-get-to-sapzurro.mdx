## ✈️ From Medellín to Sapzurro

### 🆕 Option 1A: Clic Air Direct to Capurganá ⭐⭐ MOST DIRECT (since Nov. 2025)

> **2025 update:** Clic Air relaunched direct flights **Medellín (Olaya Herrera) → Capurganá** in November 2025. This is the most direct option — you land in Capurganá and you're in Sapzurro 15 minutes later by boat.

**Step 1 — Flight: Medellín → Capurganá (50–55 minutes)**

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Airline | **Clic Air** (direct to Capurganá) |
| Medellín airport | Olaya Herrera (EOH) — city center, no need to go to Rionegro |
| Destination airport | Las Brujas (CPB) — Capurganá |
| Schedule | Check clic-air.com or authorized agencies |

**Step 2 — Boat: Capurganá → Sapzurro (15 min)**
- Cost: $25,000 COP
- Frequency: Every 30–60 minutes
- Last boat: ~5:00 PM

✅ No Acandí stopover — land directly in Capurganá
✅ Olaya Herrera is right in Medellín city center — no Rionegro trip
✅ Best option when seats are available

### Option 1B: Flight to Acandí + Boat — 2.5 hours total ⭐ RECOMMENDED

The most frequent route with spectacular Darién jungle views on the way in.

![Small plane flying over the Darién — the Acandí route](/images/avion-sancibrian.jpg)

**Step 1 — Flight: Medellín → Acandí (50 minutes)**

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Airlines | Satena, SEARCA\*, Moon Flights |
| Cost | $210,000 – $320,000 COP one way |
| Departures | Typically Tue, Thu, Sat (verify schedule) |
| Medellín airport | Olaya Herrera (EOH) — closer to downtown |
| Acandí airport | Alcides Fernández (ACD) — small, short runway |

> \***SEARCA** operates the regional aircraft, but **tickets are purchased through the agencies Nautilos or San Germán Express** — not directly from SEARCA. If you search "SEARCA tickets" online and find nothing, that's why. Go to Nautilos or San Germán Express.

**Step 2 — Taxi/moto from Acandí airport → Capurganá dock (15 min)**
- Cost: $12,000 – $17,000 COP

**Step 3 — Boat: Capurganá → Sapzurro (15 min)**
- Cost: $25,000 COP
- Frequency: Every 30–60 minutes
- Last boat: ~5:00 PM

**Why we love the Acandí route:**

✅ Most frequent — multiple departures per week
✅ You fly over the Darién jungle — spectacular aerial views
✅ Olaya Herrera airport is in the middle of Medellín — no need to travel to Rionegro
✅ Skips 1.5 hours of road between Apartadó and Turbo

⚠️ **Important notes:**
- Flights to Acandí are weather-dependent (short runway)
- Always confirm your flight 24 hours in advance
- Baggage limit: 10–15 kg (varies by airline)
- Book ahead during high season (Dec–Apr, Jul–Aug)

### Option 2: Flight to Carepa/Apartadó + Boat from Turbo — 4 hours total

A reliable backup when Acandí flights are full or cancelled.

**Step 1 — Flight: Medellín → Carepa (45 min)**

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Airlines | Satena, EasyFly |
| Cost | $175,000 – $290,000 COP one way |
| Departures | Daily in the morning |
| Medellín airport | Olaya Herrera (EOH) |
| Carepa airport | Antonio Roldán Betancourt |

**Step 2 — Road: Carepa/Apartadó → Turbo (1.5 hours)**
- Shared taxi: $35,000 COP
- Private transfer: $115,000 – $175,000 COP
- Road is in good condition

**Step 3 — Boat: Necoclí or Turbo → Capurganá → Sapzurro (1.5–2 hours)**

![The dock at Sapzurro — your arrival point](/images/sapzurro/dock.jpg)

- Cost: $70,000 – $92,000 COP
- Departures from Necoclí: ~8:00 AM (verify)
- **Main operator: el Caribe SAS** — route Necoclí → Capurganá → Sapzurro
- **Arrive early — seats fill up fast**
- Last leg Capurganá → Sapzurro: $25,000 COP (or included)

**This option is best when:**
- You can't get flights to Acandí
- You're traveling in low season (more Carepa flight frequency)
- You want more scheduling flexibility

The budget option for those with flexible schedules.

**Step 1 — Bus: Medellín → Turbo (10–12 hours, overnight)**

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Companies | Gómez Hernández, Brasilia |
| Cost | $92,000 – $140,000 COP |
| Terminal | Terminal del Norte, Medellín |
| Departures | 7:00 PM – 9:00 PM |
| Arrival in Turbo | 5:00–7:00 AM |

**Step 2 — Boat: Turbo → Capurganá → Sapzurro**

- First boat: ~8:00 AM

✅ **Pros:** Most affordable (~$115,000 total transport) · Arrive early for the first boat · No weather dependency
❌ **Cons:** Long overnight trip · Arrive tired · Road can be rough

## 💡 Essential Practical Tips

**Before you travel:**

- ✅ Confirm Acandí flights 24 hours in advance (weather can affect them)
- ✅ Bring enough cash — **no ATMs in Sapzurro**
- ✅ Fully charge your phone and power bank
- ✅ Pack sunscreen and insect repellent
- ✅ Keep your ID documents accessible
- ✅ Download your accommodation confirmation offline

**For flights to Acandí:**

- 🎒 Pack light — strict weight limits on small planes
- ⏰ Arrive at the airport early — small aircraft board quickly
- 🌤️ Have a plan B if the flight is cancelled due to weather
- 📱 Save the airline's phone number

**For the boats:**

- 🚤 Arrive at the dock 30 minutes before departure
- 🌊 Boats can be cancelled in rough seas
- 💦 Use a waterproof bag for electronics and documents
- 💊 Take seasickness medication if you're prone to motion sickness

**What to pack:**

- Small backpack or soft-sided bag (travel light)
- Comfortable shoes + sandals
- Light clothes and swimwear
- Basic first aid kit
- Zip-lock bags to protect electronics

## 📅 Best Time to Travel in 2026

**High season (December 2025 – April 2026 · July – August 2026):**
- ✅ More regular flights (fewer weather cancellations Dec–Mar)
- ✅ Calmer sea (more reliable boats)
- ✅ Less rain (Dec–Mar)
- ⚠️ Higher demand — book flights 1–2 months in advance
- ⚠️ Easter week (Semana Santa, April): very high demand — book 2–3 months ahead

**Low season (May – June · September – November 2026):**
- ⚠️ More Acandí flight cancellations possible (rainy season)
- ✅ Fewer tourists
- ✅ Better prices on flights and accommodation
- ✅ Lush green jungle at its best
- ⚠️ Some days the sea can be rough

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

**Is it better to fly to Acandí or Carepa?**
Acandí is faster and more direct, but has fewer flights and is more weather-dependent. Carepa has more options but adds 1.5 hours of road. For a first visit, we recommend Acandí if you can get a seat.

**How often do Acandí flights get cancelled?**
In dry season (Dec–Mar) it's rare. During rainy season it can happen. Satena always offers rebooking.

**Is there WiFi on the flights?**
No — these are small aircraft with no onboard WiFi.

**Do I need any vaccinations?**
None are mandatory, but yellow fever is recommended for the Darién region.

**Is it safe?**
Very safe. Sapzurro is a peaceful village with very low crime. Families with children are very welcome.

**Can I travel with kids?**
Absolutely! Children love the small planes. Sapzurro is a fantastic destination for families.

The quickest and least stressful way to reach San Blas is from Colombia.

- **2 Nights in Bahía Sapzurro (Colombia):** The ideal starting point to acclimate and receive a complete tourism and water activities orientation.

Crossing the border in rural areas like Capurganá and Puerto Obaldía can be complex due to intermittent public services and schedule changes at offices.

- **Assistance:** Personalized support is provided to expedite biometrics in Panama and passport stamping in Colombia.
- **Requirement:** You must have the booking confirmation (voucher) provided by the operator to present to Panamanian authorities.
- **Local Taxes:** Be aware of Guna Congress taxes and sector fees for island maintenance (approximately $85 USD total).

## The Step-by-Step Itinerary

This 4-night, 5-day journey is a total immersion into the ancestral Caribbean, divided into 2 nights in the tranquility of Sapzurro (Colombia) and 2 nights in the pristine paradise of Guna Yala (Panama).

![Route map from Sapzurro to San Blas](/images/blog/san-blas/map-sapzurro-san-blas.jpg)

### Day Before: Preparation in Colombia

Before crossing, we make sure everything is ready:

- **Immigration:** We complete the Colombia exit stamp one day in advance.
- **Orientation:** You'll receive a briefing on water practices, coastal geography, and an introduction to the history and local governance of the Guna people.

### Day 1: Crossing to Paradise

- **Border:** We depart Sapzurro for Puerto Obaldía (Panama) for the biometrics and immigration process.
- **Arrival at the Territory:** Boat transfer to the community of Caledonia for a traditional lunch at a host family's home.
- **Check-in:** Settlement in the cabins on Atidub Island.
- **Exploration:** Afternoon snorkeling and visit to Ogobnaga Island, ending with plankton viewing and stargazing under the night sky.

### Day 2: Cultural & Natural Immersion

- **Island Hopping:** We sail to Dargandub and Suledub islands to enjoy their reefs.
- **Guna Culture:** In the afternoon, you'll experience their traditions up close: traditional dances, cayuco (artisanal canoe) rides, and an exhibition of their traditional clothing and Molas.
- **Special Dinner:** A night of integration and special dinner with our hosts.

### Day 3: The Return

- Breakfast on the island and return to Puerto Obaldía for Panama exit procedures.
- Arrival in Sapzurro/Capurganá with assistance for the Colombia re-entry stamp.

## Critical Logistics Recommendations

- **Documentation:** Bring 3 printed copies of your passport.
- **Cash:** There are no ATMs in the border area. It's essential to bring US dollars (USD) and Colombian pesos (COP) for personal expenses, extra drinks, and handicrafts.
- **Gear:** Bring a rash guard, water sandals, flashlight, and sunscreen. Protect your belongings in dry bags for the boat transfers.

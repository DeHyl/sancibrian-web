Just 20 minutes on foot from Sapzurro, a hidden gem waits on the other side of the Colombia–Panama border: **Playa La Miel**. Turquoise water, white sand, dense jungle tumbling down to the shore — and the most scenic border crossing in the Americas. From [Cabaña Sancibrian](/en), here's your complete guide.

## What Is Playa La Miel?

La Miel ("The Honey") is a small, pristine beach tucked inside the Panamanian Darién jungle, right across the Colombian–Panamanian border. The name says it all: honey-colored waters, smooth and golden — the kind of beach you didn't think still existed.

What makes it special:

- **No road access** — only reachable on foot or by boat, keeping it beautifully unspoiled.
- **Crystal-clear water** with shallow coral reefs perfect for snorkeling.
- **Jungle to the waterline** — the Darién rainforest literally meets the sea.
- **Almost no crowds** — especially on weekdays, you may have it to yourself.
- **Panama without a long journey** — just a short hike across the border.

---

## The Hike: 20 Minutes of Pure Jungle

The trail from Sapzurro to La Miel is one of this corner of the world's quiet treasures. It starts in the center of Sapzurro village, climbs over a densely forested hill, and drops straight down to the Panamanian beach.

![Trail from Sapzurro to La Miel — through the Darién jungle](/images/activities/camino-sapzurro-la-miel.jpg)

### Trail at a Glance

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Distance** | ~1.2 km |
| **Time** | 15–25 minutes |
| **Difficulty** | Easy / moderate (one uphill section) |
| **Navigation** | Marked trail — no guide needed |
| **Best time** | Morning (before 11 AM to beat the heat) |

### Trail Tips

- **Closed-toe shoes recommended** — roots, a little mud after rain, some incline.
- Bring water — nothing available on the trail.
- The path is clearly visible; just follow the signs in Sapzurro or ask any local for "el camino a La Miel."
- In rainy season (May–November) the trail can be slippery — shoes you don't mind getting muddy.

## Crossing the Colombia–Panama Border

To enter La Miel, you need to register at the **Sapzurro border post** (Colombian migration) on the way out, and check in at the **La Miel post** (Panamanian migration) on arrival. The process is simple and free — takes just a few minutes each way.

### Documents Required

| Nationality | Required document |
|-------------|-------------------|
| **Colombian citizens** | Valid national ID card (cédula) |
| **Latin Americans (most)** | Valid passport |
| **Europeans / North Americans** | Valid passport |
| **Other nationalities** | Valid passport + check Panama visa requirements |

> **Important:** This is a day-trip border crossing. You cannot travel further into Panama via this route. Entry to La Miel is strictly for visiting the beach and returning to Colombia the same day.

### Step by Step

1. **Check out of Colombia:** Head to the Migraciones Colombia post at the start of the trail in Sapzurro. Register your exit — takes about 2 minutes.
2. **Walk the trail:** Follow the jungle path for ~20 minutes.
3. **Check into Panama:** Register at the Panamanian migration post when you arrive at the beach.
4. **Enjoy La Miel!**
5. **Return:** Register your exit from Panama and your re-entry into Colombia.

> The border post is typically open **8 AM – 4/5 PM**. Don't arrive too late — if it's closed, you won't be able to cross.

## The Beach: What to Expect

Once you cross, La Miel delivers something few travelers expect to find at this border:

### Snorkeling
La Miel's shallow reef is visible right from the surface — no boat needed. With a basic mask you'll spot tropical fish darting through coral in gin-clear water. If you're staying at Cabaña Sancibrian, ask us about snorkel gear.

### Swimming
The bay is calm and sheltered, ideal for relaxed swimming without strong currents. The water is cool and refreshing — perfect after the hike in.

### Food & Drinks: Captain Jack's and More

Once you're done swimming, the beach has a few good options to refuel.

**Captain Jack's** is the standout spot at La Miel — a pirate-themed beach bar with cold beers, tropical cocktails, and the Panamanian Caribbean breeze as your backdrop. It's the perfect place to crack open a cold one after snorkeling before heading back over the hill to Sapzurro. The vibe is laid-back and the prices are fair (everything in balboas = USD).

Beyond Captain Jack's, there are a couple of local food stalls serving simple Panamanian classics: fried fish, patacones (fried plantain), ceviche, and fresh fruit juices.

> **Tip:** Bring cash in US dollars — there's no card reader or Wi-Fi at La Miel.

### Doing Nothing
La Miel is, above all, a place to slow down. With luck you'll be nearly alone, with jungle and sea as your only company.

## Best Time to Visit

La Miel is doable year-round, but here's how the seasons play out:

| Season | Months | Conditions |
|--------|--------|------------|
| **Dry season** | December–April | ☀️ Sunny, dry trail, calm sea — ideal |
| **Light rains** | May–July | 🌤 Occasional showers, trail hikeable |
| **Heavy rains** | August–November | 🌧 Muddier trail, sea can be choppy |

> **Local tip:** Even during rainy season, mornings are usually clear. Head out before 9 AM and you'll be back before the afternoon showers.

## Getting to Sapzurro (Your Base for La Miel)

To do the La Miel hike, you first need to get to **Sapzurro**. The village is 45 minutes by boat from Capurganá, and Capurganá is reached by speedboat from Necoclí (1.5–2 hours) or by small plane from Medellín, Montería, or Quibdó.
